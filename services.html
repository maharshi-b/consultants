Complete Banking Solution

After Nationalization and mergers of the few major banks, the Indian banking Industry has passed through a phase of reforms with an aim to boost the economy, upliftment of poor and rural with an objective to serve the masses by giving more freedom and controls to boost the economy.

The Indian Banking Industry has become complex in nature after shifting from class banking to mass banking. To boost the economy the RBI / Govt. has come up with many concept such as ‘Priority Sector’ lending and many more by frequently increasing the investment and turnover limits. Latest one is as per Govt. notification number S.O.1702 (E ), dated the 1st June, 2020, the definition of Micro manufacturing and services units was increased to Rs. 1 Crore of investment and Rs. 5 Crore of turnover. The limit of small unit was increased to Rs. 10 Crore of investment and Rs 50 Crore of turnover. Similarly, the limit of medium unit was increased to Rs. 20 Crore of investment and Rs. 100 Crore of and Rs. 250 Crore of turnover.
